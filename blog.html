Decoration Guides

Design Guides
for Real Homes

Practical, honest advice on the home decor challenges most people face — small rooms, mixing patterns, rental limits, and finding your color confidence.

4 Common Decor Problems, Solved

From cramped layouts to color confusion — here are practical blueprints you can actually use.

Guide 01

Small Space, Big Style

Cramped rooms, clutter, and poor flow are among the most common home complaints. The good news is that smart furniture choices and a few layout principles can transform even the smallest apartment into a space that feels open and intentional.

1

Go vertical with storage

Use floor-to-ceiling shelves, wall-mounted desks, and hanging plants to draw the eye upward.

2

Multi‑purpose furniture

Ottomans with hidden storage, nesting coffee tables, sofa beds, and folding desks.

3

Light tones and mirrors

Stick to light neutrals, soft pastels, or a single tonal palette.

Guide 02

Mastering Pattern Mixing

Fear of clashing patterns holds many people back from creating a layered, collected look. The secret lies in balancing scale, repeating colors, and using texture as a neutral anchor.

1

Combine different scales

Pair one large‑scale print with a medium geometric and a tiny motif.

2

Texture is the neutral

Linen, wool, velvet, and jute add depth without visual noise.

3

Use a solid anchor

Start with a neutral sofa or rug. Add patterned pillows and curtains.

Guide 03

Renter‑Friendly Transformations

White walls and strict leases don't mean you have to live with a bland space. With removable products and non‑damage tricks, you can add personality while keeping your deposit safe.

1

Peel‑and‑stick everything

Removable wallpaper, backsplash tiles, and floor stickers.

2

Command hooks and tension rods

Hang curtains, art, or lightweight shelves without drilling.

3

Layered rugs & throw pillows

Transform a space instantly with soft furnishings.

Guide 04

Color Confidence: The 60‑30‑10 Rule

Choosing a cohesive color palette doesn't have to be intimidating. The 60‑30‑10 rule is a designer's secret that works for every room.

60%

Dominant color

Your walls, large furniture, or flooring. Usually a neutral.

30%

Secondary color

Accent walls, larger pieces, or textiles.

10%

Accent color

Art, pillows, plants, or decorative objects.
